The Ethics Framework

Before you open any AI tool for schoolwork, run it through this light. JRF values — discipline, integrity, courage — apply here.

🟢 Green — Go Ahead
AI is clearly enhancing your own work. Organizing your notes. Generating practice questions from your material. Prepping for an interview. Summarizing research you'll read yourself. AI as a tool, not a replacement.
🟡 Yellow — Pause & Check
It depends on the assignment, professor, and program policy. Check your syllabus before you submit. When in doubt, ask your instructor directly — that conversation shows more integrity than assuming it's fine.
🔴 Red — Full Stop
Submitting AI-generated content as your own original work when explicitly prohibited. This is academic dishonesty — and it undermines your actual learning. AI can't give you what you'll need in the interview room.

How AI Language Models Work
NLP · Tokens · Inference · LLMs

What is NLP? Natural Language Processing is the branch of AI that enables computers to understand and generate human language. Every prompt you type goes through NLP.

What is an LLM? A Large Language Model is trained on massive amounts of text to learn the statistical patterns of human language. Claude, GPT-4, and Gemini are all LLMs. They don't "think" — they predict the most likely next token given everything before it.

Tokens
Building blocks of text. ~1 token ≈ ¾ of a word. "Hello world" ≈ 3 tokens. Models have token limits per conversation.
Inference
Running the trained model to generate a response. The 2–4 second pause after you send a message is inference in real time.
Context Window
How much text the model sees at once — your entire conversation history. Staying in one chat = better, more coherent responses.
Hallucination
When an LLM generates confident-sounding but factually wrong information. Always verify high-stakes facts from primary sources.

Why this matters for your career: Interviewers now ask about AI literacy in every field. Being able to explain what an LLM does and what hallucination means puts you ahead of 90% of candidates — even in non-technical roles.

✍️
Prompt Engineering
The skill that separates AI users from AI power users

What is prompt engineering? Crafting your inputs to an AI model to get better, more specific, and more useful outputs. The prompts you used today have role, context, task, and format built in.

Role prompting
"You are a JPMorgan interviewer..." — telling the model what role to play changes the register and specificity of its response dramatically.
Context loading
Giving the model your actual information instead of asking generically. Specificity = better outputs.
Numbered tasks
Asking for a numbered list of deliverables structures the output so it doesn't ramble.
Iterative refining
Staying in the same conversation and asking follow-ups rather than starting over.
Push-back prompting
"Make that more specific. Give me concrete examples, not general advice." The model's first output is rarely its best.

The rule of thumb: A vague prompt gets a vague answer. The more real context you give — your specific situation, your specific goal — the more useful the output.

AI Literacy — Interview-Ready Q&A
Q1. What is a large language model, and how is it different from a search engine?
A large language model is an AI system trained on massive amounts of text to predict and generate human language. Unlike a search engine — which retrieves existing web pages by keyword matching — an LLM generates original responses by modeling statistical relationships between words and concepts. Search finds. LLMs synthesize and generate.
Q2. What is AI hallucination and how do you handle it professionally?
AI hallucination is when a language model generates confident-sounding but factually incorrect information. It happens because models predict plausible language, not verified facts. I handle it by treating AI output as a first draft — especially for statistics, citations, or legal and medical information — and verifying high-stakes claims against primary sources before using them professionally.
Q3. What is prompt engineering and why does it matter?
Prompt engineering is the practice of crafting AI inputs to get better, more specific outputs. A vague prompt gets a generic answer; a well-structured prompt with role, context, task, and format gets a targeted, actionable response. Key techniques include role prompting, context loading, numbered task lists, and iterative refinement within the same conversation.
Q4. What can AI do in your workflow, and what can it never replace?
AI handles information-heavy, repetitive tasks — summarizing research, drafting first versions, generating options, formatting documents. What it can't replace is judgment: knowing what's ethical, what's right for a specific situation, and how to navigate real relationships. My lived experience, character, and ability to lead under pressure are things no model can replicate. I use AI for efficiency so I can focus on impact.
